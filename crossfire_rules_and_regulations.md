## 1. Registration Regulations

### 1.1 Registration Payment/Fee

* 1.1.1 All teams must register through the official Crossfire registration form (https://docs.google.com/forms/u/0/d/e/1FAIpQLScrekuW-M7L2XLwjS2GiV07hDM2gskdh-T3ap2gkT2uNrlTJg/viewform?usp=send_form&pli=1&authuser=0).

* 1.1.2 Crossfire GG Season 4 is an **open event** - any eligible North American team may register until all 32 slots are filled. No qualification tournaments are required.

* 1.1.3 Entry fee is **$100 per team**. Entry fees are **non-refundable** for any reason.

* 1.1.4 Payment deadline is **February 23rd, 2026 @ 8:00 PM EST**. Teams failing to pay the entry fee may be removed from the tournament.

* 1.1.5 Upon registration completion, ownership of the team slot is assigned to the designated team captain.

### 1.2 Roster Submission Guidelines

* 1.2.1 During registration, teams will be prompted to designate a team captain. The team captain must be a player from the team's Crossfire core.

  * 1.2.1.1 Teams must submit a Crossfire core of 3-5 players during registration. These players are locked as the Crossfire core.

  * 1.2.1.2 Team captains may request a captain role transfer at any time, but the new captain must be part of the core.

* 1.2.2 Teams must provide complete roster information including all player names, Steam IDs, and FACEIT profiles.

* 1.2.3 All players must meet eligibility requirements:
  * Must reside in North America
  * Must not have active VAC bans or platform bans
  * Must have FACEIT Anti-Cheat installed

### 1.3 Roster Limitations

* 1.3.1 Maximum roster size: **8 players total** (5 starters, 2 substitutes, 1 coach).

* 1.3.2 Teams must play with at least **3 original core players** in every match.

* 1.3.3 **Team coaches are not permitted to substitute** as players at any point in the tournament.

* 1.3.4 **No multi-accounting** is allowed. Violations result in immediate disqualification.

* 1.3.5 **Roster changes:**
  * Allowed at any time during Group Stage (March 2-22)
  * Players added through roster changes **cannot count** toward the minimum 3 core players requirement
  * All rosters **lock once Playoff Stage begins** (March 30, 2026)
  * No roster changes permitted during Playoff Stage

### 1.4 Substitutions

* 1.4.1 **Between matches:** Teams may substitute players for any reason, provided they maintain at least 3 core players.

* 1.4.2 **During match or between maps:** Substitutions only permitted in the event of a **verifiable emergency**, subject to admin approval.

* 1.4.3 Teams must contact an admin immediately via Discord ðŸš¨â”ƒmake-a-ticket for emergency substitutions.

## 2. Tournament Format and Structure

### 2.1 Tournament Overview

* 2.1.1 **Tournament Type:** Tier 2, structured in alignment with Valve Regional Standings (VRS) competitive integrity guidelines

* 2.1.2 **Total Teams:** 32

* 2.1.3 **Prize Pool:** $3,500 USD
  * 1st Place: $2,000 USD
  * 2nd Place: $1,250 USD
  * 3rd Place: $250 USD

* 2.1.4 **Platform:** All matches hosted on FACEIT

* 2.1.5 **Server Region:** North America (United States)

* 2.1.6 Any rule updates will be publicly documented with version history and will apply equally to all participants.

### 2.2 Group Stage

* 2.2.1 **Format:** 8 Groups Ã— 4 Teams (32 teams total)

* 2.2.2 **Match Type:** Round-Robin, Best-of-3 (BO3)

* 2.2.3 **Duration:** March 2 - March 22, 2026

* 2.2.4 **Rounds:**
  * Round 1: March 2-8 (Default: Friday, March 6 @ 8 PM EST)
  * Round 2: March 9-15 (Default: Friday, March 13 @ 8 PM EST)
  * Round 3: March 16-22 (Default: Friday, March 20 @ 8 PM EST)

* 2.2.5 **Advancement:** Top 2 teams from each group advance to Playoffs (16 teams total)

* 2.2.6 Teams may reschedule matches within the round window with mutual agreement.

* 2.2.7 **Tiebreakers:** W/L Round Ratio

### 2.3 Playoff Stage

* 2.3.1 **Format:** 16-Team Single Elimination Bracket

* 2.3.2 **Match Type:** Best-of-3 (BO3)

* 2.3.3 **Duration:** March 30 - April 25, 2026

* 2.3.4 **Rounds:**
  * Round of 16: March 30 - April 5 (Default: Friday, April 3 @ 8 PM EST)
  * Quarterfinals: April 6 - April 12 (Default: Friday, April 10 @ 8 PM EST)
  * Semi-Finals: April 13 - April 19 (Default: Friday, April 17 @ 8 PM EST)
  * Grand Finals: April 20 - April 25 (Default: Friday, April 24 @ 8 PM EST)

* 2.3.5 **Break Week:** March 23-29 (No matches scheduled)

* 2.3.6 Playoff-eligible teams retain their seed throughout the playoffs.

* 2.3.7 Reschedules permitted within round window with admin approval.

### 2.4 Seeding and VRS

* 2.4.1 Seeding based on **February 2nd, 2026 VRS Leaderboard**.

* 2.4.2 **Seeding Priority:**
  1. Teams with Valve Regional Standings (VRS) rank seeded first by VRS ranking
  2. Remaining teams seeded by ESEA League Division and Win/Loss record

* 2.4.3 Final seeding list will be publicly posted before Round 1.

## 3. Online Match Rules

### 3.1 Platform Requirements

* 3.1.1 All matches and servers hosted on **FACEIT platform**.

* 3.1.2 All players required to have a **FACEIT Account**.

* 3.1.3 Standings, matches, and brackets maintained through **FACEIT Bracket**.

### 3.2 Anti-Cheat

* 3.2.1 **FACEIT Anti-Cheat is mandatory** for all Crossfire matches.

* 3.2.2 Matches will not start if a player does not have anti-cheat enabled.

* 3.2.3 Players must run FACEIT AC during all matches. Failure results in **match forfeit**.

* 3.2.4 Players with **active VAC bans or platform bans** are ineligible.

* 3.2.5 If a player is banned mid-season, **all their team's results are forfeited**.

### 3.3 Match Settings

* 3.3.1 **Freeze Time:** 15 seconds

* 3.3.2 **Round Time:** 1:55 minutes

* 3.3.3 **Regulation Start Money:** $800

* 3.3.4 **Match Format:** MR12/3

* 3.3.5 **Overtime:** MR6 with $12,500 start money, continue sides

* 3.3.6 If match must be replayed, start money will be $12,500.

## 4. Scheduling

### 4.1 Match Scheduling

* 4.1.1 **Default Match Day:** Fridays

* 4.1.2 **Default Match Time:** 8:00 PM EST

* 4.1.3 All times displayed in **EST/EDT**.

* 4.1.4 Matches must be scheduled and played between **12:00 PM and 12:00 AM EST/EDT**.

* 4.1.5 All match times are **first-come, first-served**.

* 4.1.6 Teams must communicate match times in designated Discord match chat.

* 4.1.7 Admin may set default match time if teams cannot agree on schedule.

### 4.2 Reschedules

* 4.2.1 All reschedule requests must be sent via **match chat** in Discord server.

* 4.2.2 **Group Stage:** Matches can be rescheduled up until final day of group stage with mutual team agreement.

* 4.2.3 **Playoff Stage:** Reschedules permitted within round window with **admin approval**.

* 4.2.4 Teams must submit scheduling conflicts **1 hour before match**.

### 4.3 Punctuality and Forfeits

* 4.3.1 Teams must have **5 players on server within 15 minutes** of scheduled start time.

* 4.3.2 Failure to field 5 players results in **forfeit**, scored as **13-0 win** or **2-0 win**.

* 4.3.3 If neither team can field 5 players, match may be **rescheduled** or **double forfeited**.

* 4.3.4 If no match scheduled before default time, team that shows receives **FFW (Forfeit Win)**.

* 4.3.5 **Habitual forfeits** may result in bans from future Crossfire GG seasons.

## 5. Technical Issues

### 5.1 Time-outs

* 5.1.1 **Technical Timeouts:** 1 per team (up to 15 minutes)
  * Use **!pause** command for serious technical issues
  * Teams must inform admins immediately during technical timeouts

* 5.1.2 **Tactical Timeouts:** 3 per team (30 seconds each)
  * Use vote pause for standard tactical timeouts

* 5.1.3 Teams have **10 minutes** to resolve technical issues unless otherwise permitted by admins.

* 5.1.4 Admins may pause matches at any time for technical or competitive reasons.

### 5.2 Disconnections

* 5.2.1 Teams must pause immediately if player disconnects due to technical issues.

* 5.2.2 If player cannot reconnect within timeout period, emergency substitution may be requested.

* 5.2.3 All disconnection issues must be reported to admins immediately.

### 5.3 Game Updates

* 5.3.1 After CS2 update, teams must **pause match** and contact admin.

* 5.3.2 If player cannot reconnect after CS2 update, admin must be contacted.

* 5.3.3 Admins will determine appropriate resolution for game update issues.

### 5.4 Internet/Connection Issues

* 5.4.1 After ISP outage or connection issue, teams must **pause** and contact admin.

* 5.4.2 Teams responsible for maintaining stable internet connection.

* 5.4.3 Repeated connection issues may result in forfeit at admin discretion.

## 7. Penalties

### 7.1 Cheating and Third-Party Software

* 7.1.1 **Cheating of any kind** results in immediate expulsion from Crossfire GG.

* 7.1.2 **Account sharing** during matches results in match forfeiture and expulsion.

* 7.1.3 If player banned mid-season, **all their team's results are forfeited**.

* 7.1.4 Use of unauthorized third-party programs prohibited.

* 7.1.5 **No custom player models** allowed.

### 7.2 Exploits and Illegal Actions

* 7.2.1 Exploiting known or unknown bugs for advantage is **prohibited**.

* 7.2.2 **Pixel walking, invisible ledges, or glitching** banned.

* 7.2.3 No bomb plants in **floating positions** or **through solid objects**.

* 7.2.4 Bombs must be **audible** to opposing team.

* 7.2.5 Players must not join enemy team during match.

* 7.2.6 Boosting involving **clipping is illegal**.

* 7.2.7 All infractions handled on **case-by-case basis**. Penalties can include forfeits or disqualification.

### 7.3 Communication Violations

* 7.3.1 Use of **racial slurs** results in **FFL (Forfeit Loss)**.

* 7.3.2 Second offense leads to **tournament ban**.

* 7.3.3 Public posting of disputes or admin conversations may result in penalties.

* 7.3.4 All players must uphold spirit of fair play and competition at all times.

### 7.4 Stream Sniping

* 7.4.1 At **no point** should any player or coach be in Twitch stream of match.

* 7.4.2 This includes **between maps** or during **tech pauses**.

* 7.4.3 **Zero tolerance policy** on stream sniping.

* 7.4.4 Violations result in match forfeit and potential tournament ban.

### 7.5 Betting Violations

* 7.5.1 **Betting on own matches** strictly prohibited.

* 7.5.2 Violations result in immediate disqualification and potential permanent ban.

### 7.6 Repetitive Issues

* 7.6.1 Teams with repetitive rule violations subject to escalating penalties.

* 7.6.2 Admins reserve right to remove teams with habitual violations.
